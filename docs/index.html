Performance of the Xpert Xpress Strep A for detection of &lt;i&gt;Streptococcus pyogenes&lt;/i&gt; from pediatric pleural fluids.

Amaral G, Gadkar VJ, Almodahka A et al.. Performance of the Xpert Xpress Strep A for detection of &lt;i&gt;Streptococcus pyogenes&lt;/i&gt; from pediatric pleural fluids.. Microbiology spectrum. 2026

This study evaluated the diagnostic performance of the Xpert Xpress Strep A, a rapid quantitative PCR assay originally designed for throat swabs, for detecting *Streptococcus pyogenes* from pediatric pleural fluids in the context of invasive Group A streptococcal pneumonia. The researchers compared Xpert results against a composite reference standard comprising bacterial culture and a laboratory-developed qPCR assay targeting a different gene, using a convenience sample of pleural fluid specimens from pediatric patients. Xpert achieved perfect agreement with the composite reference standard and with the laboratory-developed qPCR, detecting all positive specimens, whereas culture alone identified only a minority of those same positive cases. The findings demonstrate that Xpert is substantially more sensitive than culture for identifying *S. pyogenes* in pleural fluid, likely because many patients had received antibiotics before sample collection. The authors conclude that Xpert offers both improved diagnostic accuracy and faster turnaround time, supporting its utility for guiding clinical management and public health responses to invasive Group A streptococcal disease.

Silver Nanoparticles in Antibacterial Research: Mechanisms, Applications, and Emerging Perspectives

Hasan Karataş, Furkan Eker, Emir Akdaşçi et al.. Silver Nanoparticles in Antibacterial Research: Mechanisms, Applications, and Emerging Perspectives. International Journal of Molecular Sciences. 2026

Silver nanoparticles have attracted significant research interest as potential alternatives to conventional antibiotics, particularly given the global rise of drug-resistant pathogens. This review examines the antibacterial mechanisms of silver nanoparticles, which include the release of silver ions that disrupt bacterial cell membranes, generation of reactive oxygen species, inhibition of protein synthesis, and interference with DNA replication. The physicochemical properties of these nanoparticles, including their shape, size, and surface characteristics, are identified as key determinants of their efficacy and specificity against a broad range of bacteria including antibiotic-resistant strains. The review also surveys their practical applications across agricultural, biomedical, and environmental sectors, while acknowledging that inappropriate dosing or suboptimal administration can result in significant toxicity, representing a major challenge for their safe use. The authors conclude by discussing future perspectives for optimizing silver nanoparticle use in antibacterial contexts.

Ask, Solve, Generate: Self-Evolving Unified Multimodal Understanding and Generation via Self-Consistency Rewards

Ritesh Thawkar, Shravan Venkatraman, Omkar Thawakar et al.. Ask, Solve, Generate: Self-Evolving Unified Multimodal Understanding and Generation via Self-Consistency Rewards. arXiv. 2026 preprint

This paper investigates whether a unified large multimodal model capable of both visual understanding and image generation can improve itself using only unlabeled images, without relying on human annotations or external reward models. The authors propose a self-evolving training framework built around three internal roles: a Proposer that generates visual questions, a Solver that answers and evaluates them, and a Generator that synthesizes images, with training driven entirely by self-derived consistency signals. To stabilize training, they introduce a token-level prediction uncertainty measure called Solver Token Entropy as a continuous difficulty signal, and design a multi-scale internal evaluation scheme for image generation that couples question-answer fidelity with cycle-consistent captioning. The framework is architecture-agnostic, demonstrated across diffusion-based, rectified-flow, and autoregressive model families using only each backbone's native interface. The method consistently improves visual understanding across multiple benchmarks and also enhances image generation quality, demonstrating that unified multimodal models can self-improve both capabilities autonomously.

DnA: Denoising Attention for Visual Tasks

Ron Campos, Subhajit Maity, Xin Li et al.. DnA: Denoising Attention for Visual Tasks. arXiv. 2026 preprint

This paper investigates the limitations of standard softmax-based multihead attention in vision transformers, arguing that conventional softmax produces noisy attention patterns that dilute relevant features and reduce model performance. The authors propose Denoising Attention (DnA), a method that introduces both a positive query to identify class-relevant image features and a negative query to capture closely associated but irrelevant features, then projects these interactions into two distinct subspaces with larger principal angles to improve separation and discriminability. DnA is evaluated on image classification, video understanding, and video large language models using a vision transformer backbone, demonstrating consistent performance improvements across all tasks. Extensive empirical analyses are provided to justify the design choices, particularly the use of two interacting subspaces and the denoising mechanism.

Don't Settle at the Mode! Mitigating Diversity Collapse in Pretrained Flow Models via Feature Self-Guidance

Pradhaan S Bhat, Rishubh Parihar, Abhijnya Bhat et al.. Don't Settle at the Mode! Mitigating Diversity Collapse in Pretrained Flow Models via Feature Self-Guidance. arXiv. 2026 preprint

This paper addresses the problem of diversity collapse in flow-based generative models, where generating multiple images from the same text or image prompt tends to produce outputs that cluster around a single mode rather than exploring the full range of plausible outputs. The authors propose a training-free, plug-and-play self-guidance mechanism that works by dispersing the internal features of the flow model during batch generation, without relying on any external reward models or incurring substantial computational overhead. To prevent these dispersed features from drifting off the data manifold, a manifold regularization step is introduced to project them back onto the manifold, maintaining alignment with the input conditioning. The method integrates directly into pretrained flow models as a drop-in module with minimal added inference cost. Experiments across multiple conditional generation tasks, including text-to-image, depth-to-image, and reference image generation in both multi-step and few-step settings, demonstrate meaningful improvements in sample diversity without sacrificing output fidelity.

Dirac fermions in non-Hermitian magnetic fields: Zero modes and index theorem

Christopher A. Leong, Bitan Roy. Dirac fermions in non-Hermitian magnetic fields: Zero modes and index theorem. arXiv. 2026 preprint

This paper investigates non-Hermitian (NH) extensions of Dirac theory in which a spatially modulated anti-Hermitian mass-like term acts as a NH gauge field, generating effective NH magnetic fields. The authors show analytically that when a planar Dirac system encloses a finite flux of such NH magnetic fields, a set of spatially localized zero-energy eigenmodes emerges that are purely right-handed or left-handed, extending the conventional Atiyah-Singer index theorem to the non-Hermitian setting. These zero modes and their associated flat bands are confirmed numerically through microscopic simulations on graphene's honeycomb lattice, providing a concrete lattice realization. The paper discusses potential experimental platforms for observing these effects and argues that the resulting NH zero-mode flat bands could serve as a foundation for phenomena such as NH magnetic catalysis, NH fractional topological phases, and NH chiral anomaly.

Reinforcement Learning without Ground-Truth Solutions can Improve LLMs

Yingyu Lin, Qiyue Gao, Nikki Lijing Kuang et al.. Reinforcement Learning without Ground-Truth Solutions can Improve LLMs. arXiv. 2026 preprint

This paper investigates whether large language models can be improved through reinforcement learning on optimization tasks that lack ground-truth solutions, a setting where standard reward-based training approaches typically fail. The authors introduce a framework called RiVER that uses deterministic execution feedback as continuous-valued supervision and applies group-relative reinforcement learning with calibrated reward shaping to address two identified failure modes: scale dominance, where score magnitude differences across instances distort learning, and frequency dominance, where commonly sampled but suboptimal solutions overshadow rare high-quality ones. RiVER was trained on heuristic contest programming tasks and evaluated on several coding benchmarks spanning both score-based and exact-solution problem types. The framework meaningfully improved performance on the score-based benchmark but, crucially, also transferred to exact-solution benchmarks despite never training on tasks with ground-truth answers, while baselines using raw execution scores failed to achieve this transfer. These findings suggest that score-based optimization tasks with properly calibrated rewards can serve as effective training environments for developing general coding ability in the absence of ground-truth solutions.

The paper studied whether synthetic epitopes derived from Group B Streptococcus proteins could serve as the basis for a novel ELISA-based immunodiagnostic test to detect GBS carriage in pregnant women, offering an alternative to the current gold standard of bacterial culture, which is slow and can yield ambiguous results. The researchers identified three epitopes from the GBS proteins GroEL, enolase, and EF-Tu, produced them as synthetic peptides, and used them individually and in combination as ELISA antigens to detect IgG, IgM, and IgA antibodies in serum samples from GBS-positive and GBS-negative pregnant women. All three peptides, whether used alone or mixed, significantly distinguished between the two groups, with the enolase- and EF-Tu-derived epitopes performing best individually due to their high immunoreactivity at lower concentrations. The authors conclude that this epitope-based ELISA represents a promising and potentially pioneering diagnostic prototype with no current equivalent on the market.

Bridging the diagnostic gap: the role of culture methods in the era of syndromic panels for central nervous system infections.

This study evaluated the diagnostic performance of a syndromic molecular panel for central nervous system infections by comparing it against conventional culture methods using cerebrospinal fluid samples collected over three years at a national reference center. The researchers assessed concordance, sensitivity, specificity, and predictive values between the two approaches across a large dataset of paired results. The syndromic panel demonstrated high specificity and strong negative predictive value when restricted to its included bacterial targets, but overall sensitivity was substantially lower due to pathogens falling outside the panel's coverage. Conventional culture identified clinically relevant organisms not targeted by the panel, including certain staphylococcal and streptococcal species as well as rare environmental pathogens, underscoring its indispensable complementary role. The authors conclude that syndromic molecular panels should not be used as standalone diagnostics and that optimal management of central nervous system infections requires systematic integration of both approaches, alongside development of next-generation platforms with broader pathogen coverage.

Utility and clinical relevance of multiplex PCR panels for diagnosis of central nervous system infections.

Gürler M, Karaoğlu Ç, Yurtcu EE et al.. Utility and clinical relevance of multiplex PCR panels for diagnosis of central nervous system infections.. The Indian journal of medical research. 2026

This study evaluated the real-world diagnostic performance and clinical utility of multiplex polymerase chain reaction panels designed to detect multiple pathogens simultaneously from cerebrospinal fluid in patients with suspected meningitis or encephalitis. Researchers retrospectively examined cerebrospinal fluid samples tested over a five-year period at a tertiary care hospital, reviewing test results alongside clinical data and subsequent management decisions. Viral pathogens dominated the positive detections, with human herpesvirus-6, enterovirus, and herpes simplex virus type-1 being most frequently identified, while Streptococcus pneumoniae was the most common bacterial finding. Despite the panels' capacity for rapid pathogen identification, fewer than half of positive results were associated with a documented change in diagnosis or treatment, and repeat testing after an initial negative result offered minimal additional diagnostic yield. The authors conclude that inappropriate test ordering and insufficient clinician familiarity with panel limitations substantially diminish the clinical impact of these tools, calling for clearer testing criteria and improved awareness to enhance diagnostic efficiency.

Severe community-acquired pneumonia: current concepts and controversies

Pierre-François Dequin, A Torres, M. Bassetti et al.. Severe community-acquired pneumonia: current concepts and controversies. Intensive Care Medicine. 2026

Severe community-acquired pneumonia remains a significant public health burden due to its frequency, complications, and treatment costs. The paper reviews current understanding and ongoing controversies in the management of this condition, including the challenge of selecting appropriate empirical antibiotic therapy in the context of resistant pathogens and the emerging but incompletely evaluated role of rapid molecular diagnostic techniques. Treatment duration is trending shorter and may be guided by clinical markers or biomarkers, while the potential for immunomodulatory therapy has gained attention given that the condition involves dysregulated systemic inflammation.
