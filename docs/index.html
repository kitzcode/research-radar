This review examines the molecular mechanisms by which World Health Organization priority bacterial pathogens have evolved resistance to newly approved antibiotics. The authors systematically catalogued resistance strategies against fifteen antibiotics approved over a recent multi-year period, spanning diverse drug classes including β-lactam combinations, tetracycline derivatives, pleuromutilins, aminoglycosides, and fluoroquinolones.
