Recent Advances in Microfluidic Chip Technology for Laboratory Medicine: Innovations and Artificial Intelligence Integration

Hong Cai, Dongxia Wang, Yiqun Zhao et al.. Recent Advances in Microfluidic Chip Technology for Laboratory Medicine: Innovations and Artificial Intelligence Integration. Biosensors. 2026

This review examines recent advances in microfluidic chip technology, commonly known as lab-on-a-chip systems, and their growing role in laboratory medicine and clinical diagnostics. The authors conducted a comprehensive survey of innovations in chip design and fabrication—including three-dimensional printing, modular architectures, and flexible substrates—alongside progress in integrating advanced biosensors capable of detecting circulating tumor cells, nucleic acids, and exosomes. A central focus is the convergence of microfluidics with artificial intelligence and machine learning, which has enabled the development of smart diagnostic platforms offering self-optimization, automated analysis, and clinical decision support. These combined technologies have expanded applications across cancer diagnostics, infectious disease detection, point-of-care testing, personalized medicine, and emerging areas such as neuroscience diagnostics and microbiome profiling. The review also identifies ongoing challenges in manufacturing scalability, clinical validation, and system integration that must be addressed before next-generation microfluidic platforms can be broadly translated into routine clinical and public health practice.

Severe community-acquired pneumonia: current concepts and controversies

Pierre-François Dequin, A Torres, M. Bassetti et al.. Severe community-acquired pneumonia: current concepts and controversies. Intensive Care Medicine. 2026

Severe community-acquired pneumonia remains a significant public health burden due to its frequency, complications, and treatment costs. The paper reviews current understanding and ongoing controversies in the management of this condition, including the challenge of selecting appropriate empirical antibiotic therapy in the context of resistant pathogens and the emerging but incompletely evaluated role of rapid molecular diagnostic techniques. Treatment duration is trending shorter and may be guided by clinical markers or biomarkers, while the potential for immunomodulatory therapy has gained attention given that the condition involves dysregulated systemic inflammation. Recent large randomized trials of corticosteroids have produced conflicting results, leaving unresolved which patient subgroups — potentially those with marked inflammatory responses but not those with influenza — might benefit, and some macrolides are also being explored for immunomodulatory properties. The review concludes that supportive care, especially tailored respiratory support, remains a cornerstone of management and must be individualized to disease severity.

A Novel Voltammetric Point-of-Care Device for Rapid and Accurate Nucleic Acid and Viral Pathogen Detection.

Munita R, Lyttleton R, Danefur E et al.. A Novel Voltammetric Point-of-Care Device for Rapid and Accurate Nucleic Acid and Viral Pathogen Detection.. ACS omega. 2026

This paper developed and evaluated a novel electrochemical diagnostic platform that combines isothermal nucleic acid amplification with real-time voltammetric detection for identifying viral pathogens. The system detects amplification-associated proton release using redox probing of a pH-sensing compound, offering an alternative to optical detection methods that can impede conventional isothermal approaches like LAMP. The platform was tested against multiple respiratory viruses including SARS-CoV-2, influenza A and B viruses, and RSV A using RNA purified from clinical samples, demonstrating high concordance with RT-qPCR as the reference standard. An extraction-free proof-of-concept workflow was also validated using equine nasal swab samples for a veterinary pathogen, with correct classification of all tested samples. The authors conclude that real-time voltammetric LAMP is feasible across diverse sample types and pathogens, though they acknowledge that broader point-of-care validation in additional matrices and prospective cohorts is still needed.

Trends of nucleic acid - based point-of-care diagnostics for infectious diseases.

Hatem H, Mysara M, Ramadan R. Trends of nucleic acid - based point-of-care diagnostics for infectious diseases.. Journal of biological engineering. 2026

This review paper examines the landscape of nucleic acid-based point-of-care diagnostics for infectious diseases, motivated by the global need for rapid, decentralized testing outside conventional laboratory settings. The authors organized their analysis around the three core operational steps of such diagnostics: nucleic acid extraction, amplification, and detection. They surveyed extraction strategies including magnetic bead-based, paper-based, and integrated microfluidic approaches, emphasizing designs that remain simple, portable, and equipment-free. Amplification methods reviewed include both thermal cycling approaches such as PCR and isothermal alternatives such as LAMP, RPA, and NASBA, all of which have been adapted for low-power and portable conditions. On the detection side, the review covers a spectrum from colorimetric and fluorescent lateral flow assays to advanced biosensor platforms exploiting CRISPR/Cas systems and toehold switch mechanisms, which offer highly sensitive and programmable nucleic acid recognition.

A nested PCR-based point-of-care multiplex test for detection of bacterial pathogens in cerebrospinal fluids.

Wu J, Zhao Z, Pang P et al.. A nested PCR-based point-of-care multiplex test for detection of bacterial pathogens in cerebrospinal fluids.. Frontiers in cellular and infection microbiology. 2026

This study developed and evaluated a multiplex one-tube nested real-time PCR assay integrated with a point-of-care testing instrument for detecting three bacterial pathogens — *Streptococcus pneumoniae*, *Streptococcus agalactiae*, and *Streptococcus pyogenes* — directly from cerebrospinal fluid. The approach was designed to automate the entire workflow from sample input to result output, eliminating the need for bacterial culture. The assay demonstrated very low detection limits and no cross-reactivity with other common pathogens, and it reduced total testing time compared to manual extraction paired with conventional real-time PCR. When evaluated on clinical cerebrospinal fluid and non-cerebrospinal fluid samples, the point-of-care method identified additional positive cases that conventional real-time PCR had missed. The authors conclude that this integrated system offers a rapid, sensitive, and operationally streamlined approach to diagnosing bacterial meningitis-related pathogens in cerebrospinal fluid.

Isothermal nucleic acid amplification techniques: A comprehensive overview on their principle &amp; applications as alternative to PCR.

Amare Amdiyee A, Sisay Tessema T. Isothermal nucleic acid amplification techniques: A comprehensive overview on their principle &amp; applications as alternative to PCR.. Practical laboratory medicine. 2026

This review paper examined isothermal nucleic acid amplification methods as alternatives to conventional PCR-based diagnostics. The authors surveyed the principles, detection methods, and applications of the most widely used techniques, including loop-mediated isothermal amplification, helicase-dependent amplification, rolling circle amplification, strand displacement amplification, nucleic acid sequence-based amplification, and recombinase polymerase amplification. Unlike PCR, these methods operate at a constant temperature and do not require thermal cycling equipment, making them particularly suited for resource-limited settings that demand rapid, affordable, and simple nucleic acid detection. The review addresses a gap in the existing literature by comprehensively consolidating reaction mechanisms, product detection strategies, and clinical diagnostic applications in a single work. The authors conclude that isothermal amplification techniques offer significant promise for broadening access to molecular diagnostics across research and clinical practice, and they discuss future perspectives for the field.

Cotelici AM, Bălășoiu AT, Boldeanu MV et al.. Improving the Precision of Etiological Diagnosis in Bacterial Infections Using Molecular Technologies: A Comparative Analysis of Platforms, AI Integration, and Point-of-Care Deployment.. International journal of molecular sciences. 2026

This review examines the landscape of molecular diagnostic technologies for bacterial infections, motivated by the inadequacies of conventional culture-based methods and the growing threat of antimicrobial resistance. The authors conduct a structured comparative analysis of major platforms, including PCR and its variants, isothermal amplification, next-generation sequencing, CRISPR-based diagnostics, and digital PCR, evaluating each against parameters such as sensitivity, specificity, detection limits, turnaround time, and multiplexing capability. The review also critically assesses how artificial intelligence and machine learning are being integrated into molecular diagnostic workflows to support antimicrobial resistance prediction and clinical decision-making. Additionally, the translational feasibility of these technologies for point-of-care deployment is considered, accounting for clinical validation requirements and real-world operational constraints. The authors conclude by outlining an integrated perspective on current molecular strategies and identifying key challenges and future directions for advancing culture-independent bacteriological diagnostics.

A Multiplex One-Tube Nested Real-Time PCR Assay for the Point-of-Care Testing of Infectious Meningitis.

Zhang D, Wang J, Zhao Z et al.. A Multiplex One-Tube Nested Real-Time PCR Assay for the Point-of-Care Testing of Infectious Meningitis.. Pathogens (Basel, Switzerland). 2026

This study developed a multiplex one-tube nested real-time PCR assay integrated with a portable, fully automated point-of-care testing platform for detecting three key meningitis pathogens in cerebrospinal fluid. The assay performs nested amplification within a closed system using conventional primers and probes, reducing contamination risk and operational complexity, and was evaluated both analytically and clinically using simulated and real cerebrospinal fluid samples. The method demonstrated very low detection limits, no cross-reactivity against a broad panel of non-target microorganisms, and delivered results in under two hours. Clinical testing against conventional quantitative PCR revealed near-perfect agreement, with the new assay additionally detecting more cases of one fungal pathogen, suggesting enhanced sensitivity under real-world conditions. The authors conclude that the platform offers a rapid, sensitive, and operationally straightforward approach for infectious meningitis diagnostics, though they note that validation in larger patient cohorts is needed.

Acute pancreatitis: mechanisms and therapeutic approaches

Qian Hu, Yue Hu, Chunlu Tan et al.. Acute pancreatitis: mechanisms and therapeutic approaches. Signal Transduction and Targeted Therapy. 2026

This review paper examines acute pancreatitis, a severe inflammatory condition whose global incidence is rising with notable regional variation in both frequency and etiology, including gallstone disease, hypertriglyceridemia, and alcohol abuse. The authors synthesize current knowledge on disease definition and classification, as well as advances in clinical scoring systems, biomarkers, and artificial intelligence-based predictive models intended to support early risk stratification and clinical decision-making. The review also details the multilevel pathophysiological mechanisms underlying the condition, encompassing aberrant enzymatic activation, calcium overload, impaired autophagy, inflammatory cascades, and various modes of pancreatic acinar cell death. From a therapeutic standpoint, the paper covers early- and late-stage management strategies, adjunctive approaches drawn from traditional Chinese medicine, and individualized treatment considerations for special patient populations. Emerging interventions such as nanotechnology and extracellular vesicle-based therapies are additionally highlighted as promising future directions for research and clinical innovation.

Advanced Technologies in Extracellular Vesicle Biosensing: Platforms, Standardization, and Clinical Translation

Seong-Jun Choi, Jaewon Choi, Jin Kim et al.. Advanced Technologies in Extracellular Vesicle Biosensing: Platforms, Standardization, and Clinical Translation. Molecules. 2026

This review examines the current landscape of biosensing technologies designed to detect and analyze extracellular vesicles, which carry molecular cargo that reflects the biological state of their parent cells and thus hold strong potential as noninvasive diagnostic biomarkers. The authors survey a range of detection modalities—including surface plasmon resonance, electrochemical, fluorescence, and magnetic systems—evaluating how each has advanced sensitivity and specificity for EV analysis. The review also addresses efforts to standardize EV research through established guidelines and analytical validation frameworks, and explores how multiplexed profiling at the single-vesicle level combined with machine learning can improve diagnostic accuracy and account for biological heterogeneity. Clinical applications across cancer, neurological, cardiometabolic, and infectious diseases are discussed to illustrate translational progress. The authors conclude by identifying outstanding challenges in manufacturing, quality control, and point-of-care implementation, and propose a unified framework intended to integrate EV biosensing into precision diagnostics and personalized medicine.

Feasibility of integrating a point of care triage test into routine antiretroviral therapy monitoring in Mozambique: a qualitative evaluation

Myburgh H, Saura-Lázaro A, van den Bogaart E et al.. Feasibility of integrating a point of care triage test into routine antiretroviral therapy monitoring in Mozambique: a qualitative evaluation. 2026

This qualitative study explored perceptions of integrating a point-of-care triage test measuring interferon-gamma-induced protein 10 (IP-10), a chemokine correlated with HIV viral load, into routine antiretroviral therapy monitoring in Mozambique. Researchers conducted multiple rounds of interviews with people living with HIV undergoing IP-10 testing, healthcare providers, and HIV programme stakeholders across different health system levels, nested within a clinical evaluation at two primary healthcare facilities. Participants widely valued viral load monitoring but described significant barriers to access, including laboratory delays and health system constraints. The IP-10 triage test was broadly acceptable, with same-day results perceived to reduce patient anxiety, support treatment adherence, and enable timely clinical decision-making by identifying those needing confirmatory viral load testing. However, concerns about test accuracy and the continued necessity of confirmatory testing were raised, with successful implementation deemed dependent on adequate training, workflow integration, quality assurance, and clear patient communication.

Implementation bottlenecks of near point of care HIV viral load monitoring for children and young people in Tanzania: A Qualitative Study.

Msoka P, Mtenga A, Maro R et al.. Implementation bottlenecks of near point of care HIV viral load monitoring for children and young people in Tanzania: A Qualitative Study.. PloS one. 2026

This qualitative study investigated the implementation bottlenecks of near point-of-care HIV viral load monitoring for children and young people living with HIV in Tanzania, from the perspective of healthcare workers. The researchers conducted clinic observations and in-depth interviews with healthcare workers at multiple time points across Tanzanian intervention sites participating in a larger cluster-randomised controlled trial, using a thematic framework analysis guided by the Measurement Instrument for Determinants of Innovations. The findings revealed that near point-of-care testing improved result turnaround time and enabled same-day counselling, which motivated both healthcare workers and patients, and was further supported by compatibility with existing clinical practices, teamwork, and management commitment. However, significant barriers were identified, including patients waiting extended periods for results, scarcity of physical resources such as rooms and electricity, staff shortages, and delays caused by sample transport to nearby laboratories. The authors conclude that realising the full potential of near point-of-care viral load monitoring requires targeted investment in staff training, infrastructure, sample handling procedures, and alignment with clinical guidelines.

Baseline Data on Viral Suppression, Treatment Adherence and Mental Health in Peripartum Girls and Young Women With HIV in Zimbabwe.

Chawana-Mutingwende TD, Ngara B, Mudadi LS et al.. Baseline Data on Viral Suppression, Treatment Adherence and Mental Health in Peripartum Girls and Young Women With HIV in Zimbabwe.. Journal of the International AIDS Society. 2026

This study examined viral suppression, treatment adherence, drug exposure, and mental health among peripartum adolescent girls and young women living with HIV in Zimbabwe. Participants aged fifteen to twenty-four years who were in the peripartum period and on tenofovir-based antiretroviral therapy were enrolled into a randomized controlled and observational cohort study, with point-of-care viral load testing and urine tenofovir detection used alongside validated mental health screening tools. The researchers found high rates of viral suppression at baseline, with the large majority of participants achieving undetectable viral loads, and suppression was strongly associated with detectable tenofovir in urine and higher self-reported adherence. Socioeconomic factors including food security, financial security, and marital status were also significantly associated with viral suppression. Mental health outcomes were generally positive, with most participants showing minimal depression and anxiety, though moderate HIV-related stigma was common among over half the cohort.

Feasibility and reliability of decentralized HIV-1 viral load monitoring on self-sampled blood.

Mendes de Leon KF, Jurriaans S, Klungers RD et al.. Feasibility and reliability of decentralized HIV-1 viral load monitoring on self-sampled blood.. PloS one. 2026

This study evaluated whether home-based blood self-sampling using a capillary collection device could reliably support HIV-1 viral load monitoring as an alternative to clinic-based testing. Participants recruited at an outpatient clinic collected their own blood samples at home, which were then tested for HIV RNA and compared against conventional plasma samples from the same individuals, with additional stored low-level viremic samples also assessed. While overall correlation between self-sampled and conventionally sampled results was acceptable, a substantial proportion of self-collected samples had insufficient plasma volume for analysis, and low-level viremia was missed in several cases where conventional testing detected detectable virus. User-friendliness was rated positively by only around half of participants, and overall return rates were modest. The authors conclude that meaningful technical and procedural improvements are needed before this approach can be considered reliable for routine clinical use.

Herpes Simplex Virus Type 2 Screening in Persons with and Without HIV: Evidence, Challenges, and Future Directions

Laith J. Abu-Raddad, Hiam Chemaitelly, Anna Wald et al.. Herpes Simplex Virus Type 2 Screening in Persons with and Without HIV: Evidence, Challenges, and Future Directions. Current HIV/AIDS Reports. 2026

This review examined the current evidence and guidelines surrounding serologic screening for herpes simplex virus type 2, particularly in the context of HIV prevention and care. The authors synthesized existing guidance, test performance data, available therapeutics, and psychosocial considerations to evaluate the role of screening across different populations. International guidelines recommend against routine population-level serologic screening in asymptomatic individuals, citing poor test specificity, lack of curative or highly potent antiviral options, absence of demonstrated population-level benefit, and potential psychosocial harms from diagnosis. Instead, evidence supports targeted screening in defined clinical contexts, such as among people living with HIV, sex partners of infected individuals, certain pregnant women, and those with recurrent or atypical symptoms, where results can directly guide management. The authors conclude that advances in diagnostics, novel antivirals, and therapeutic or curative vaccines may eventually shift the risk-benefit balance to justify broader routine screening, but such a shift requires first demonstrating measurable reductions in disease burden or transmission at the population level.

AI-Enhanced Point-of-Care Diagnostics for Infectious Diseases in Resource-Limited Settings: A Scoping Review.

Farquhar H. AI-Enhanced Point-of-Care Diagnostics for Infectious Diseases in Resource-Limited Settings: A Scoping Review.. Tropical medicine & international health : TM & IH. 2026

This scoping review systematically mapped research on artificial intelligence-enhanced point-of-care and rapid diagnostic technologies for infectious diseases in resource-limited settings, focusing on disease coverage, geographic representation, and validation quality. Following established scoping review methodology, the authors searched five major databases for studies published over approximately a decade and included over two hundred studies after screening. The field was found to be growing rapidly, with nearly half of included studies published in the most recent period, and dominated by work on COVID-19, malaria, and tuberculosis, while neglected tropical diseases were substantially underrepresented. Convolutional neural networks were the most commonly employed artificial intelligence approach, applied across modalities including microscopy, molecular diagnostics, biosensors, and rapid diagnostic tests. Despite the growth of the field, the review identified critical gaps in validation rigor—with only a small fraction of studies reporting prospective field validation—as well as geographic concentration in East Africa and South Asia that leaves West Africa and Latin America underrepresented, underscoring the need for broader disease coverage, rigorous field validation, and greater involvement of end-user communities.
