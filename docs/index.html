This scoping review systematically mapped the research landscape of artificial intelligence-enhanced point-of-care and rapid diagnostic technologies for infectious diseases in resource-limited settings, focusing on disease coverage, geographic representation, and validation quality. Following established scoping review methodology, the authors searched five major databases spanning over a decade of publications, screening over a thousand records and ultimately including more than two hundred studies. This paper addresses the challenge of making accurate survival predictions when data are interval-censored, a problem that is especially difficult under wide censoring intervals and limited follow-up as commonly seen in chronic disease research. The authors developed a novel transfer learning method called SPOT-IC that borrows survival probability information from external source studies to improve estimation in a target study, without requiring access to individual-level source data or imposing strict assumptions about similarity between source and target models. The method transfers information through a specially designed penalty term and uses an EM algorithm for efficient computation, while a data-adaptive aggregation procedure handles the case of multiple source studies with unknown informativeness, protecting against negative transfer. Theoretical results establish that the proposed estimator achieves a faster convergence rate than using only target data whenever at least one source study is sufficiently informative. Simulation studies and an application to Alzheimer's disease neuroimaging data confirm the practical effectiveness of the approach.

The authors introduce a scalable diagnostic framework with two complementary metrics—an order-statistics expressivity score that benchmarks a reservoir ensemble's largest output probabilities against a theoretical Haar random baseline without reconstructing full output distributions, and an effective rank measure of the feature matrix that captures how much input-dependent information is available for the classical readout layer.
